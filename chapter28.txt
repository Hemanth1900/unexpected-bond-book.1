Silence is strange.

When it comes from strangers, it feels normal. When it comes from someone familiar, it feels loud.

That week, Charan felt it everywhere.

In class, when Aadhya sat two rows ahead and didn’t turn back. In the corridor, when they passed each other with only a small nod. In the hostel at night, when his phone stayed quiet longer than usual.

Karti noticed first.

“You guys fought?”

“No.”

“Then why this ‘formal formal’ behaviour suddenly?”

Charan didn’t answer.

Because he didn’t know.

Nothing had happened.

And maybe that was the problem.

There was no moment to fix.

Just a slow, quiet drifting.

And drifting is harder to stop than breaking.
