The Distance That Didn’t Make Sense

Distance didn’t arrive dramatically.

There was no argument. No moment that could be pointed at and named as the reason.

It was just… different.

Messages became shorter. Calls happened less often. Conversations ended without stretching into the night like they used to.

Nothing had broken.

And yet something had shifted.

Charan noticed it in the spaces.

The pauses between replies. The way she stopped sharing small details. The way she sounded present… but slightly elsewhere.

He told himself she was busy.

Assignments. Family. Life.

But the truth sat quietly beneath that comfort —

he was the one who had stepped back first.

Not intentionally. Not consciously.

But enough.

And now the distance existed between them like something neither of them wanted to name.
