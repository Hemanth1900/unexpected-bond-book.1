The terrace felt colder that night.

Not because of the weather.

Because of the thoughts.

Charan sat alone, watching the campus lights flicker in the distance.

People were laughing somewhere below. Someone was playing music loudly. A group walked past, arguing about something trivial.

Life was continuing.

But inside him, something had paused.

He replayed conversations. Moments. Expressions.

Her laughter. Her pauses. The way she listened. The way she understood without asking.

And suddenly…

the idea of her not being there felt heavier than anything he had faced before.

Not dramatic.

Not painful.

Just… empty.

That’s when fear arrived.

Not of losing her.

But of realising what she meant before it was too late.
