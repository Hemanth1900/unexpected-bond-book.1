
It didn’t happen in a grand moment.

No music. No confession. No sudden clarity like the movies promise.

Just an ordinary day.

Classes. Noise. Assignments. People moving around like always.

And somewhere in the middle of it, Charan noticed something simple.

Every decision he made included her somewhere.

Every thought circled back to her presence. Every memory of the past year had her in it — quietly, naturally, consistently.

She hadn’t forced her way into his life.

She had grown into it.

And now…

removing her from it felt impossible.

That evening, he sat alone, phone in his hand, staring at their last conversation.

He didn’t type.

Didn’t call.

Didn’t move.

He just… felt it.

Fully.

For the first time without denial.

Without distraction. Without running from it.

All the small moments. All the quiet days. All the ordinary conversations.

They weren’t small.

They had been building something.

Something he had refused to name.

Until now.

His chest felt heavy. Not with sadness.

With truth.

And the truth arrived quietly.

Not as a thought.

As a realisation.

Unavoidable. Simple. Clear.

He closed his eyes.

And finally admitted it to himself.

—

**I love her.**
