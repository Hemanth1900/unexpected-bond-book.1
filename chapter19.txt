

Some things stay safe as long as they remain unspoken.

The moment someone else notices them… they stop being private.

That’s when you start seeing them differently.

That’s when you start changing.

—

It happened on a normal day.

No festival. No late-night talks. No emotional moment.

Just a regular class.

The kind where half the students were listening, half pretending, and the rest waiting for the bell like it would rescue them from existence.

I was sitting with Karti, flipping through notes, when someone dropped into the seat in front of us.

Rohit.

Not close. Not distant either. Just one of those people who spoke to everyone casually.

He turned halfway toward me.

“Charan.”

“Hmm?”

He smirked.

“You and Aadhya… something going on ah?”

The words landed too easily.

Too casually.

Like they didn’t mean anything.

But they did.

Karti choked on his water beside me.

I stared at Rohit.

“What?”

He laughed. “Relax da. Just asking.”

“There’s nothing.”

“Okay, okay,” he said, raising his hands. “Don’t get defensive.”

“I’m not defensive.”

“Sure.”

He turned back toward the front like the conversation was over.

Like it hadn’t just rearranged something inside me.

—

I didn’t look at her for the rest of the class.

Not intentionally.

But I became… aware.

Aware of where she was sitting.

Aware of how often we spoke.

Aware of how it might look from outside.

And that awareness made everything feel exposed.

—

After class, she walked up beside me like she always did.

Natural. Unforced.

“You understood the last topic?” she asked.

“Yeah.”

“You’ll send notes?”

“Hmm.”

I kept my answers short.

She noticed.

Of course she did.

“You okay?” she asked.

“Yeah.”

“You sound like someone forced you to talk.”

“I’m just tired.”

She looked at me for a second longer than usual.

Then nodded slowly.

“Okay.”

We walked together till the corridor split.

Usually, one of us would say something random.

A joke. A complaint. A useless observation.

This time… silence.

Not comfortable.

Just… unfamiliar.

—

Karti didn’t wait.

The moment we reached the hostel, he dropped his bag and looked straight at me.

“What happened in class?”

“Nothing.”

“Rohit’s comment got to you.”

“It didn’t.”

He stared.

“Bro. I know your face better than you do.”

I leaned against the wall, exhaling slowly.

“It just felt… weird.”

“Why?”

“Because now it feels like everyone can see it.”

“See what?”

I didn’t answer.

He smiled slightly.

“Exactly.”

I shook my head. “There’s nothing to see.”

“Then why are you acting like you got caught?”

That question stayed longer than anything else he’d said.

Because I didn’t have an answer.

—

That night, our conversation felt… different.

Not distant.

Just cautious.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Finished dinner?”

<strong>Me:</strong> “Yeah.”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Assignment done?”

<strong>Me:</strong> “Almost.”

Pause.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “You’re being quiet today.”

I stared at the message.

Then typed.

Deleted.

Typed again.

<strong>Me:</strong> “Just tired.”

Three dots appeared.

Disappeared.

Appeared again.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Okay.”

That was it.

No push.

No questions.

And somehow… that hurt more.

—

Days passed.

Nothing dramatic.

No fights. No misunderstandings. No distance you could measure.

But something had shifted.

I spoke less.

Observed more.

Stopped reacting instinctively.

Because now I kept hearing that line.

“You and Aadhya… something going on ah?”

It echoed in random moments.

During class.

During lunch.

While walking back.

And slowly, I started pulling back.

Not intentionally.

Just… carefully.

—

She noticed.

Not immediately.

But eventually.

One evening, while leaving class, she walked beside me quietly.

Then asked,

“Did I do something?”

I stopped.

“What?”

“You’ve been… different.”

I forced a small smile.

“Different how?”

“You talk less. You avoid eye contact. You reply late. You don’t even argue with me anymore.”

I didn’t know what to say.

Because everything she said was true.

And none of it was deliberate.

“I’ve just been busy,” I said.

She watched me carefully.

“Hmm.”

That sound wasn’t agreement.

It was observation.

Then she said softly,

“Okay.”

And walked ahead.

—

I stood there for a second longer.

Because for the first time…

there was distance.

Not physical.

Not emotional.

But something subtle.

Something that hadn’t existed before.

Awareness.

The kind that makes you careful with every word.

The kind that stops you from being natural.

The kind that creates space even when you’re standing side by side.

—

That night, I lay awake again.

Thinking about something I didn’t want to admit.

Maybe connections don’t break because of fights.

Maybe they change because of attention.

Because the moment people start noticing…

you start protecting it.

Hiding it.

Overthinking it.

And in that process…

you stop being yourself inside it.

Maybe that’s what I was doing.

Not pulling away from her.

Pulling away from how easy it used to feel.

And maybe…

that was the first real risk.

Not losing her.

But changing the version of us that existed before anyone noticed.

Because once something becomes visible…

it can never return to being simple again.
