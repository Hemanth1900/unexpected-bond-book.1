Chapter 1
The Weight of Quiet Things

I don’t remember when things started changing inside me.
There wasn’t a specific day… no dramatic incident… no loud heartbreak.

Just a slow shift.

Like a room getting darker even though the light is still on.

People still laughed around me. Conversations still happened. Life still moved at the same speed for everyone else.
But inside… something had started sitting quietly. Watching. Thinking. Feeling more than it should.

And I didn’t know how to explain it.

Charan — that’s my name.
If someone asks what kind of person I am, I won’t know what to say. I’m not the loudest in the room. Not the funniest. Not the strongest.
But I feel things deeply. Too deeply sometimes.

I notice small things.

The way someone’s tone changes.
The silence after a message is seen.
The difference between being around people… and actually being with someone.

I learned early that not every feeling needs words.
Some just stay. Sit in your chest. Grow roots.

Most days I act normal.
Talk normally. Smile normally. Respond normally.

But there’s always another version of me running in the background —
thinking… replaying… questioning…

“Was that real?”
“Did they mean it?”
“Why do I care this much?”

I never told anyone this, but…
I’ve always felt like I exist more in my thoughts than in the world.

And maybe that’s where everything started.

Not with a person.
Not with love.
Not with pain.

Just… with me.

I used to think everyone felt this way.

That everyone overthought conversations after they ended.
That everyone replayed moments before sleeping.
That everyone felt a strange heaviness for no clear reason.

But slowly, I realized… most people move on faster.

They laugh, forget, replace, continue.

I stay.

Not physically — but mentally.
I stay in moments. In words. In expressions people didn’t even realize they showed.

And the worst part?

I get attached to things that were never mine.

A late-night conversation.
A random act of care.
Someone remembering small details about me.

For them, it might have been normal.
For me, it became something I carried for days.

Sometimes I wonder if my heart just doesn’t know how to behave casually.

It either feels everything…
or nothing at all.

There’s no in-between.

And that’s dangerous.

Because when you feel everything, even silence becomes loud.
Even distance starts sounding like rejection.
Even a small change in someone’s tone feels like something breaking.

I never show this side though.

People know me as calm.
Balanced.
Easy to talk to.

They don’t see the noise inside.

They don’t see how many versions of conversations I create in my head.
How many endings I imagine before things even begin.
How I prepare myself to lose people… before they even decide to stay.

Maybe that’s why I never fully let go.

Or maybe…
I was just waiting for someone who would make all this noise feel understood.

Not fixed.
Not judged.

Just… understood.

I wasn’t always like this.

There was a time when life felt simple.
When happiness didn’t need a reason.
When attachment didn’t feel like a risk.

Back then, people came and went… and I didn’t notice the leaving.

Now, I notice everything.

The delay in replies.
The way someone slowly stops asking about your day.
The shift from long conversations… to dry words… to silence.

Silence says a lot.
More than anger ever could.

And I’ve learned to sit inside that silence.

Not fight it.
Not question it out loud.

Just… feel it.

There’s a strange loneliness in being the one who cares more.
You don’t complain, because technically nothing is wrong.
No one hurt you directly.
No one said they were leaving.

But you feel the distance anyway.

And it changes you.

You become quieter.
More observant.
Less expressive.

You start protecting your heart without even realizing it.

You laugh the same.
Talk the same.
Act the same.

But inside, you stop expecting.

Because expectations… are where most things break.

I don’t know when I started becoming this version of myself.

But now that I am here…
I can’t go back to being unaware.

And maybe that’s the real problem.

Once you understand your own emotions deeply…
you can’t pretend anymore.

You start seeing love differently.
People differently.
Even yourself… differently.

And that’s when life stops feeling like a simple story…

…and starts feeling like something much heavier.

Some nights are harder than others.

Not because something happened that day…
but because nothing happened at all.

Those are the nights when my mind refuses to stay quiet.
It searches for memories. Replays old conversations.
Finds faces I tried to forget… and places I never really left.

I lie there, staring at the ceiling,
feeling everything at once and nothing at the same time.

It’s strange how a person can be surrounded by people all day…
and still feel alone when the lights go off.

That’s when the real thoughts come out.

Questions I never ask anyone.

“Am I too much?”
“Do I care more than I should?”
“Why do I get attached so easily?”

No one ever taught me how to feel less.
How to detach.
How to stop my heart from holding onto moments like they’re permanent.

And honestly… I don’t even know if I want to.

Because even though it hurts sometimes…
these feelings are the only proof that I’m alive inside.

That I’m not empty.
That I still care.
That I still hope.

Hope.

That dangerous, quiet thing.

It makes you believe someone will understand you someday.
That one person will look at you… and see beyond the calm face…
and notice the chaos you hide so carefully.

Not to fix it.

But to sit beside it.

To stay.

I don’t know when that person will come into my life.
Or if they already have, and I just didn’t notice.

But I know one thing for sure.

The day someone truly understands me…
without me having to explain every piece of my silence…

that day will change everything.

Because the truth is…

Moving on isn’t really about loving someone new.
It’s about waking up and going through your day without wondering if someone, somewhere, is still thinking about you.

And I haven’t reached that place yet.

There are battles inside me no one will ever fully understand.
Fights I had quietly. Nights I survived silently.
Smiles I wore just to make it through the day.

Sometimes I feel proud of myself for surviving.
Other times… I feel tired of always being the one holding everything together.

Late nights are the worst.

Memories don’t knock before entering.
They just sit beside you and start speaking in familiar voices.

And suddenly, you’re back in moments you thought you left behind.

Some people don’t stay forever.
But the space they occupy inside you doesn’t leave easily.

You carry it.

In songs.
In random days.
In quiet pauses between conversations.

And slowly… it becomes part of who you are.

I’ve learned something painful about people.

If you keep holding space for someone who doesn’t hold space for you…
you start losing yourself in the process.

Not loudly.
Not suddenly.

Slowly.

You begin to feel drained.
Unseen.
Replaceable.

And still… you stay.

Because your heart remembers what it felt like when they cared.

But maybe… not everything is meant to be fixed.

Maybe some things are meant to be understood… and left behind.

Because the past was never meant to live inside the present.
And it definitely wasn’t meant to scare you about the future.

Still, I overthink.

I imagine things breaking before they actually do.
I prepare for endings even when something is just beginning.

And that’s the hardest part about living inside your head —
sometimes the pain you feel isn’t from what happened…

but from what you’re afraid might happen.

And yet… despite everything…

a part of me still waits.

For someone who won’t let conversations end unfinished.
Who won’t let me sleep with a heavy heart after an argument.
Who won’t make me feel unheard when I’m trying to stay.

Not perfection.

Just presence.

Just effort.

Just someone who chooses to stay when leaving would be easier.

Because sometimes… late at night…

I still remember things.

And those memories don’t just stay in my mind.

They reach my chest.
My throat.
My eyes.

And I lie there quietly…
letting the tears fall where no one can see them.

That’s the thing about pain.

It doesn’t always scream.

Sometimes…
it just sits beside you in silence…

…and becomes a part of you.
