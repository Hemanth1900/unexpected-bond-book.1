

Some shifts don’t happen in a single moment.

They begin in the background — in the way conversations stretch a little longer, in the way silence feels less empty, in the way certain people start occupying more space in your day without asking for it.

That week didn’t feel different at first.

Classes resumed like they always did. Assignments piled up. The hostel corridor stayed noisy at night. Someone was always shouting, laughing, arguing, dragging chairs, complaining about deadlines as if the world had personally betrayed them.

Everything felt normal.

And yet… something had started moving underneath it.

It began with exhaustion.

The semester had picked up pace faster than anyone expected. Lab submissions, internal tests, attendance warnings, project group meetings that went nowhere — everything overlapping until days felt shorter than they actually were.

I was in the corridor outside class, leaning against the wall, half-reading notes and half-staring at nothing.

Karti dropped beside me with a dramatic sigh.

Karti: Bro, if they add one more assignment, I’m dropping out and opening a tea stall.  
Me: You say that every semester.  
Karti: This time I mean it.  
Me: You meant it last time too.

He stared at me for a second, then smirked.

Karti: Okay fine. I won’t drop out. But I will complain professionally.

I let out a small laugh.

Karti: You’re quieter these days.  
Me: Am I?  
Karti: Hmm. You think a lot now before talking. Earlier you just said whatever came to mind.  
Me: Maybe I finally matured.  
Karti: Or… someone’s voice is playing in your head constantly.

I didn’t respond.

He grinned like he’d just confirmed his theory.

Karti: Yeah. That face. Exactly that face.  
Me: Shut up.

He laughed and stood up as the class doors opened.

But his words stayed.

Because they weren’t entirely wrong.

Aadhya had become part of my day in ways I hadn’t planned.

Not loudly. Not dramatically.

Just… consistently.

Morning messages that weren’t even conversations. Just random thoughts.  
Short exchanges during breaks.  
Longer talks at night when everything else went quiet.

It wasn’t dependence.

But it wasn’t casual either.

It felt like… alignment.

Like our days were slowly learning how to move beside each other.

That evening, I was sitting on the hostel terrace, trying to revise.

The sky was turning orange, then slowly fading into blue. Someone downstairs was playing music too loudly. A group near the water tank was arguing about football.

My phone buzzed.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> Did you finish the assignment?  
Me: Half.  
<strong>Aadhya:</strong> Same. I keep getting distracted.  
Me: By?  
<strong>Aadhya:</strong> Life. Overthinking. Hunger. Everything.  
Me: Valid reasons.  
<strong>Aadhya:</strong> You?  
Me: Overthinking.  
<strong>Aadhya:</strong> About?

I stared at the screen longer than I should have.

Me: Nothing important.

She replied after a pause.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> You always say that when it’s important.

I smiled.

She knew.

Not everything. But enough.

The next day, during class, Bhagya appeared again.

She slid into the seat beside ours, placing her notebook on the desk.

Bhags: Charan, did you note the last readings properly? I missed one page.

Her tone was casual, focused. No hesitation.

I nodded and passed my notebook.

Karti: Bro, you’ve become academic support for half the class.

I ignored him.

Bhags: Thanks. I’ll return it after copying.

She looked up briefly, smiled — quick, polite — then got back to writing.

Nothing unusual.

And yet… I noticed.

That night, Karti and I were walking back from the canteen.

Karti: So… Aadhya.  
Me: Again?  
Karti: Just asking.  
Me: About what?  
Karti: You two talk daily, right?  
Me: Yeah.  
Karti: And?  
Me: And what?

He stopped walking and turned toward me.

Karti: Do you even realise how your face changes when her name pops up?  
Me: What?  
Karti: You relax. Like… instantly.

I didn’t know what to say to that.

Because I hadn’t noticed.

But hearing it from someone else made it feel… real.

Days passed like that.

College. Hostel. Conversations. Submissions. Laughter. Small irritations. Random moments of peace.

But something subtle had begun.

I started noticing when Aadhya was quiet.

Not absent. Just… quieter than usual.

Shorter replies. Longer gaps.

Nothing alarming.

But enough to make me pause.

And in those pauses, my thoughts drifted.

Was she tired?  
Busy?  
Or just… slowly pulling away without realising?

I didn’t ask.

Because asking would mean admitting I cared.

And I wasn’t ready for that.

That’s when it truly hit me.

Things were shifting.

Not dramatically. Not painfully.

But steadily.

Aadhya had become emotional gravity.  
Karti remained the anchor.  
Bhagya… an unexpected new presence.  
College… the stage where everything quietly unfolded.

And me?

I was standing somewhere in the middle.

Trying to understand what all of it meant.

Trying to understand what I was becoming.

Because for the first time in a long while…  
my world didn’t feel controlled.

It felt alive.

Messy. Unpredictable. Growing.

And maybe that was the real change.

Not her.  
Not them.  
Me.

I was no longer just observing life.

I was starting to feel it move.
