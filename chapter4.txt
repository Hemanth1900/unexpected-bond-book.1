

No important event.
No sudden emotional moment.
Nothing that could be marked as the beginning.

Just a night like any other.

The kind where the world slows down without asking. Where conversations feel softer, and thoughts feel louder. I wasn’t doing anything different — just existing inside another ordinary evening.

And then she messaged.

Nothing unusual. Nothing urgent.

Just something small.

But for some reason, the conversation stayed longer than usual. It didn’t end where it normally would. It moved slowly, like neither of us was in a hurry to leave.

There was no effort in it.

No searching for the right words.
No thinking about how to respond.

Everything just… came.

And that’s when I noticed something.

She didn’t talk to fill silence.

She let it exist.

There were pauses between messages. Not awkward ones. Not forced. Just quiet spaces where neither of us felt the need to say something immediately.

And strangely, those pauses felt comfortable.

Like sitting beside someone and not needing to prove you belonged there.

We talked about small things.

Nothing emotional.
Nothing heavy.

Just fragments of our day. Things that wouldn’t matter to anyone else. Things I usually wouldn’t even mention.

But with her, they didn’t feel insignificant.

They felt… shareable.

And she listened.

Not in a way that exaggerated interest. Not with big reactions or forced curiosity. Just simply. Naturally. Like every word had a place.

At one point, I typed something and erased it.

Then typed again.

Then erased it again.

I didn’t even know why.

Maybe because I wasn’t used to being honest without protecting myself first.

But she noticed.

Not directly.

She just asked,

“Why do you stop halfway sometimes?”

It wasn’t a deep question.

But it felt like one.

Because no one had pointed that out before. No one had noticed the hesitation between my words.

For a moment, I didn’t reply.

And she didn’t push.

That was new.

Most people fill silence with assumptions. With guesses. With more words.

She didn’t.

She waited.

And in that waiting, I felt something I hadn’t in a long time.

Space.

Not distance.

Just… space to exist without explanation.

So I answered.

Not fully. Not completely honest. But more than I usually allowed myself to be.

And she understood.

Not because I explained well.

But because she didn’t try to interpret beyond what I said.

She accepted it as it was.

That kind of understanding feels quiet. It doesn’t overwhelm you. It doesn’t make you emotional in the moment.

It just settles somewhere inside you.

And stays.

We kept talking.

Time passed without either of us noticing. The conversation didn’t feel long. It didn’t feel intense.

It just felt natural.

Like something that had always been there — and we had only just started paying attention.

At some point, the world around me faded.

The room was still. The night had grown deeper. And the only thing holding my attention was that conversation.

Not because it was important.

But because it felt real.

And somewhere in between those ordinary messages, I realised something.

I wasn’t talking to pass time.

I was staying.

Staying in that moment.
Staying in that conversation.
Staying because I wanted to.

Not out of habit.

Out of comfort.

She didn’t try to be impressive. Didn’t try to sound perfect. Didn’t try to keep the conversation alive forcefully.

She just stayed.

And I stayed with her.

That’s when it shifted.

Not loudly.

Not dramatically.

Just quietly.

From talking… to connecting.

From presence… to familiarity.

From comfort… to something that felt like the beginning of trust.

I didn’t name it.

Didn’t question it.

Didn’t even think too much about it.

I just let it exist.

Because for the first time, a conversation didn’t feel like something temporary.

It felt like something that would continue tomorrow.

And the day after that.

And maybe longer.

Before we stopped talking, there was a pause again.

Not awkward.

Just… soft.

And in that pause, I realised something I hadn’t admitted to myself before.

I was looking forward to hearing from her again.

Not in a way that demanded it.

But in a way that felt certain.

Like the night would come.
Like the day would end.
Like some things just happen without needing to be asked.

She had become one of those things.

And for the first time…

her presence didn’t just feel necessary.

It felt real.
