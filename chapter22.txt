
Some things don’t come back all at once.

Not after distance.
Not after overthinking.
Not after silence.

They return in pieces.

A familiar joke.
A longer pause that doesn’t feel awkward.
A conversation that stretches without effort.

That’s how it began again.

Slowly.

—

The next few days felt… lighter.

Not dramatic.

Not like everything had magically fixed itself.

Just easier.

She started sending random messages again.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “I think our lab assistant secretly hates us.”

<strong>Me:</strong> “He hates everyone.”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “No. Special hate for our batch.”

<strong>Me:</strong> “We deserve it.”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Rude.”

That single word stayed longer than the conversation itself.

Because it felt like before.

Normal.

Unfiltered.

—

In class, we spoke the way we used to.

Not cautiously.

Not calculating every word.

Just naturally.

She leaned over during lectures again to whisper doubts.

I argued with her over useless points again.

Karti, of course, noticed immediately.

“Finally,” he muttered one afternoon.

“What?” I asked.

“You stopped acting like you’re attending a funeral.”

I smirked slightly.

“Shut up.”

He grinned. “Good. Back to normal.”

But even he knew.

It wasn’t exactly the same normal.

It was something… deeper.

—

That evening, we ended up on the terrace again.

Not planned.

Not arranged.

Just both arriving at the same time like muscle memory had guided us there.

The sky was darker than usual. Clouds covering most of the light. The wind stronger.

She leaned against the railing.

“You stopped avoiding eye contact,” she said.

I laughed softly.

“You noticed that?”

“I notice everything.”

“That’s scary.”

She shrugged. “Someone has to.”

Silence followed.

But it wasn’t empty.

Just… calm.

—

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “You know… I missed this.”

I looked at her.

“What?”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Talking without feeling like something is about to break.”

The honesty in her voice hit me again.

“Me too,” I admitted.

—

We stayed there longer than we intended.

Talking about random things.

How fast the semester was moving.
How hostel food somehow tasted worse every week.
How Karti would probably fail if sarcasm were graded.

And somewhere between those conversations, comfort settled back in.

Not fragile.

Not uncertain.

Just… present.

—

Later, while leaving, she said casually,

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Arjun’s annoying.”

I raised an eyebrow. “Oh?”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “He keeps talking during lectures.”

“You were laughing a lot that day.”

She rolled her eyes.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Because he says stupid things. Not because he’s funny.”

I nodded slowly.

“Good clarification.”

She looked at me, amused.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Jealous?”

I scoffed. “No.”

She didn’t argue.

Just smiled slightly like she already knew the answer.

—

That night, I realised something.

Comfort isn’t about talking constantly.

It’s about knowing the connection still exists… even when you’re quiet.

And that connection had returned.

Not loudly.

But steadily.

—

Days began flowing again.

Classes.

Assignments.

Canteen breaks.

Terrace conversations.

And through all of it, she stayed… constant.

Not demanding.

Not distant.

Just there.

Like before.

But stronger.

—

One afternoon, during group study, she leaned closer while explaining something.

Her hand brushed mine accidentally.

She didn’t move away immediately.

Neither did I.

The moment passed in seconds.

But it lingered.

Neither of us mentioned it.

But both of us noticed.

And that awareness stayed.

Not uncomfortable.

Just… new.

—

Later that night, while messaging, she suddenly asked,

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Do you ever think about where all this is going?”

The question came out of nowhere.

Or maybe… it had been building for a while.

I stared at the screen.

Then typed slowly.

<strong>Me:</strong> “Sometimes.”

Pause.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “And?”

I exhaled.

<strong>Me:</strong> “I don’t want to rush it.”

Three dots.

Disappeared.

Came back.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Me neither.”

That was it.

No dramatic follow-up.

No confession.

Just… agreement.

And strangely, that felt more meaningful than anything else we could have said.

—

Lying in bed later, I thought about something Karti had once said.

“You relax when her name pops up.”

He was right.

But now it wasn’t just relaxation.

It was familiarity.

Trust.

And something deeper… slowly forming beneath it all.

Not love yet.

Not something I could name.

But something that felt… inevitable.

Because some connections don’t push forward loudly.

They grow quietly.

Until one day, you realise they’ve become part of your everyday life.

And losing them would feel like losing a part of yourself.

And maybe…

that’s when they become real.
