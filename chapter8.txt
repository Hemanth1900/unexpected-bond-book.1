

Some changes don’t arrive loudly.
They don’t announce themselves.
They don’t demand attention.
They slip in quietly, sit beside you, and only later do you realise something feels… different.

That’s how that week began.

Not with anything important.
Just another set of classes, another routine falling into place.
But slowly, new patterns started forming.



It began during a lab session.

The class was divided into small groups, as usual. People shuffled around, dragging stools, arguing about equipment, pretending to understand instructions they clearly hadn’t listened to.

I was with Karti, trying to figure out whether the machine in front of us even worked.

“That wire isn’t even connected da,” he muttered.

“Don’t touch anything. It might start working suddenly.”

He snorted.

That’s when someone leaned slightly closer and asked,
“Charan, do you have the readings from last time?”

I turned.

She stood beside our table, holding her phone, waiting patiently.

“I shifted sections,” she added. “Mine didn’t transfer.”

I searched my files and sent them.

“Thanks,” she said, then paused briefly.
“Bhagya.”

The nickname clicked.

Bhags.

I had heard it earlier. Lab noise. Someone calling from across the room.

Now it had a name.

I nodded. “Charan.”

“I know,” she smiled lightly. “Attendance list.”

And she moved back to her group.

That was the first time I really noticed her.

Not because she tried to be noticed.
But because interaction makes people real in a way distance never does.

She wasn’t loud. Didn’t linger. Didn’t over-explain.

She just asked, waited, left.

Simple.

Natural.

And somehow… memorable.

Karti leaned closer immediately.

“Academic partnership starting ah?”

“Lab readings,” I said.

“Of course.”

He didn’t believe me.

But neither did I think it meant anything.

Because it didn’t.

At least… not yet.



The next day, corridor.

“Charan, micro class shifted to second hour ah?”

I turned before thinking.

Bhagya.

“Yeah.”

She nodded once and moved on.

Short. Practical.

Nothing personal.



Later, during break—

“Charan, can you forward that pdf once?”

“Charan, submission date confirmed ah?”

Each conversation small. Academic. Expected.

The kind that shouldn’t matter.

And yet, repetition builds familiarity faster than intensity ever does.

Soon, I recognised her voice without looking.

Not intentionally.

Just… automatically.



Karti noticed first, obviously.

“Bro,” he muttered during class, “Bhags full academic support only ah?”

“Lab work,” I said.

He leaned back dramatically. “Of course. Pure education.”

I ignored him.

Because it was nothing.

Just classmates talking.



But someone else noticed too.

Aadhya.

She didn’t turn back. Didn’t react. Didn’t ask.

But awareness settles into small pauses.

The way her pen slowed when my name came from behind.

The way her attention flickered once, then returned to whatever she was doing.

Nothing visible.

Nothing dramatic.

Just… noticing.



Canteen break.

Karti and I sat at our usual table.

Halfway through a conversation, Bhagya appeared with a tray.

“Can I sit?”

Karti immediately pulled a chair. “Of course.”

She laughed softly and sat.

Conversation flowed easily — labs, professors, internals, the confusion of new schedules.

She spoke naturally, without hesitation.

Not loud. Not quiet.

Just present.

Karti matched her energy instantly.

I mostly listened.

At some point, I looked toward the counter.

Aadhya stood there with her friends.

For a brief second, her eyes moved in our direction.

Then she looked away.

That moment should’ve meant nothing.

But it stayed.



“Popularity increasing,” Karti whispered later as we walked back.

“Stop,” I muttered.

He laughed.

“First Aadhya. Now Bhags.”

“There is no ‘first Aadhya.’”

He raised his hands, grinning.

But I didn’t argue further.

Because everything still existed in a space without labels.



Evening.

Class ended. People began leaving in clusters.

I stepped out when Aadhya fell into step beside me, like she always did.

“Assignment started?” she asked.

“Not yet.”

“Same.”

A few steps passed.

Then she added, casually, “You and… Bhags seem to be talking a lot these days.”

Her tone wasn’t sharp.

Not teasing.

Not accusing.

Just observational.

I blinked. “Lab mostly. She shifted sections.”

“Hmm.”

That was all.

No reaction.

But the air shifted slightly.

Subtly.



We reached the corridor split.

She turned toward the exit.

I turned toward the stairs.

“See you tomorrow,” she said.

“Yeah.”

She walked away.

And I stood there a second longer than necessary.

Because something had changed.

Not loudly.

Not dramatically.

Just enough to be felt.



That night, Karti was talking non-stop about internals and hostel cricket.

I listened, half present.

Because my mind kept replaying a single moment.

Not lab.

Not canteen.

Just one line.

“You and… Bhags seem to be talking a lot these days.”

Simple.

Normal.

But it stayed.

Because for the first time, I realised something.

Aadhya wasn’t just part of my routine anymore.

She was noticing it.

And once someone starts noticing your place in their world…

You start noticing theirs too.

And that’s where things begin to shift.

Even when nothing has happened yet.
