

There’s always a phase before something changes.

Not the moment itself.
Not the confession.
Not the decision.

Just… the build-up.

Where everything feels normal on the outside,
but inside, every thought starts pointing toward the same direction.

That’s where I was.



The days after the festival felt unusually clear.

Not lighter.

Not heavier.

Just… aware.

I noticed her more.

Not just when she spoke.

When she listened.

When she paused before replying.

When she looked away after holding eye contact a second longer than usual.

Every detail felt sharper.

More important.



Karti noticed too.

We were sitting on the hostel floor, pretending to study, when he suddenly said,

“You’re gone.”

“What?”

“Mentally. Emotionally. Spiritually.”

“Stop being dramatic.”

He leaned forward.

“You’re thinking about telling her, right?”

The words hit directly.

I didn’t respond.

He grinned slowly.

“Yeah. Thought so.”

“I didn’t say anything.”

“You didn’t have to.”

He leaned back, satisfied.

“Just don’t mess it up by overthinking.”

I let out a small laugh.

Too late for that.



That evening, I saw her near the library steps.

Alone.

Scrolling through her phone.

The sun was setting behind her, casting a warm glow across the campus.

I walked toward her.

She looked up.

Smiled immediately.

Not polite.

Not formal.

Natural.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Done with pretending to study?”

“Done with pretending I understand anything.”

She laughed.

And just like that… the world felt simpler again.



We sat there for a while.

Talking about nothing.

Random observations.

Stupid complaints.

The kind of conversations that don’t need direction.

And yet, beneath all of it…

something was waiting.



<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “You’ve been… different.”

I smirked. “Again?”

She nodded.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Not distant. Just… clearer.”

“Clearer?”

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Like you’re finally accepting something instead of running from it.”

That line made my chest tighten.

Because she had seen through everything.

Without me saying a word.



“Maybe,” I admitted.

She looked at me carefully.

Not pushing.

Just present.



Silence settled.

But not the uncomfortable kind.

The kind where both people know something important is sitting between them.

Waiting.



<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “You know you don’t have to say anything before you’re ready, right?”

I looked at her.

“What makes you think I’m planning to?”

She smiled slightly.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Because you’re terrible at hiding things from me.”

I laughed softly.

“Fair.”



We stayed there till the campus lights flickered on.

Students moved past.

Evening turned into night.

And still…

neither of us said it.

But both of us knew.

It was coming.



That night, I couldn’t sleep.

Not because I was confused.

But because I finally wasn’t.

For months, everything had been unspoken.

Undefined.

Safe in its ambiguity.

Now…

it felt like the moment was approaching.

And for the first time…

I wasn’t afraid of it.



Because I realised something simple.

I didn’t just enjoy talking to her.

I didn’t just care about her presence.

I didn’t just feel comfortable around her.

I looked forward to her.

Missed her when she wasn’t around.

Noticed when she was quiet.

Felt relief when she laughed.

Felt unsettled when something felt off.

And that wasn’t habit.

That wasn’t routine.

That was something deeper.

Something real.



The next day, while walking to class, she fell into step beside me like always.

No effort.

No hesitation.

Just natural.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “What are you thinking?”

I glanced at her.

Then looked ahead.

“Something I’ll probably tell you soon.”

She didn’t ask what.

Didn’t push.

Just nodded slowly.

<strong>Aadhya:</strong> “Okay.”

And that “okay” felt like permission.

Not pressure.

Not expectation.

Just space.



Classes passed.

People talked.

Assignments piled up.

Life continued.

But inside…

everything felt aligned.

Like all the scattered thoughts of the past months were finally pointing toward one truth.

One moment.

One sentence.



Standing alone on the terrace that night, looking at the city lights flicker in the distance, I realised something quietly.

The fear was gone.

Not completely.

But enough.

Because the feeling had grown stronger than the hesitation.

And sooner or later…

it would come out.

Not as a dramatic confession.

Not as a rehearsed line.

Just… naturally.

Like everything else between us had.

And when it did…

everything would change.

Not immediately.

Not loudly.

But permanently.

Because some things can’t go back to being unnamed once they’re spoken.

And I was finally ready to speak them.

Maybe not today.

Maybe not tomorrow.

But soon.

Very soon.

And for the first time…

I was waiting for that moment.

Not running from it.
